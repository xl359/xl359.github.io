About

I am a PhD student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Michigan. My research lies at the intersection of optimization, control, and machine learning, with applications in power and energy systems.

My work focuses on integrating data-driven models with optimization frameworks, including electricity markets, data center energy systems, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Research Interests

  • Optimization and control for power systems
  • Machine learning for energy systems
  • Electricity markets and dynamic pricing
  • Optimization over trained neural networks
  • Multi-agent and distributed control

Experience

  • University of Michigan – Teaching Assistant (Power System Design and Operation)
  • Honda Aircraft Company – Optimization Intern

Selected Projects

  • Online electricity market design using primal–dual dynamics
  • Optimization over trained neural networks (difference-of-convex)
  • Bayesian neural networks via convex reformulation
  • Neural network pruning using optimization
